# Multiple Sclerosis

**Platform link**: Target genes **EXTL2** (platform signed-DE rank 43, Open Targets genetic-association score 0.12) and **UBAC1** (platform signed-DE rank 44, Open Targets genetic-association score 0.38).

## 1. Definition & Population
Multiple sclerosis (MS) is a chronic immune-mediated demyelinating disease of the central nervous system, driven classically by CD4+ T-cell-initiated autoimmune attack on myelin, with growing recognition of B-cell contributions. It most often presents in young adults, with a female predominance.

## 2. Epidemiology Highlights
- The Atlas of MS (3rd edition), a global compendium tracking country/region-level epidemiology, documented a rising worldwide prevalence trend; the paper's title itself states this: "Rising prevalence of multiple sclerosis worldwide: Insights from the Atlas of MS, third edition".
- The same Atlas effort relies on country representatives reporting standardized epidemiologic survey data to build global/regional/country prevalence estimates "Country representatives reported epidemiologic data and their sources via survey".

## 3. Biology Highlights
- EXTL2 (a glycosyltransferase) has direct, disease-relevant literature: a study found EXTL2 promotes proteoglycan deposition and neuroinflammation after demyelination, explicitly linking it to the MS model EAE — "Chondroitin sulfate proteoglycans (CSPGs)...have also been highlighted as a modulator of macrophage infiltration into the central nervous system in experimental autoimmune encephalomyelitis, an inflammatory model of multiple sclerosis", with the study interrogating SNPs near EXTL2 in this context.
- UBAC1 returned zero direct PubMed hits for "UBAC1 multiple sclerosis" — not established in this pass, despite UBAC1's known genetic association score (0.38) in Open Targets.
- Broader MS pathogenesis research continues to refine the CD4+ T-cell vs. B-cell balance: a 2026 review on EAE modeling notes "Historically, MS animal models of experimental autoimmune encephalomyelitis have featured B cell-independent disease driven by CD4 T cells activated against central nervous [myelin antigens]", situating the platform's CD4+ T-cell perturb-seq approach within the field's dominant disease model.

## 4. Current Treatment
- Ocrelizumab (OCREVUS), an anti-CD20 B-cell-depleting monoclonal antibody, is FDA-approved for MS — Drugs@FDA BLA761053 shows original approval action dated 2017-03-28, with a follow-on subcutaneous co-formulation (OCREVUS ZUNOVO, BLA761371) approved 2024-09-13.
- The broader disease-modifying therapy (DMT) landscape (interferons, glatiramer acetate, S1P modulators, other anti-CD20s, natalizumab) is well established in the field but not itemized with a specific source in this pass.
