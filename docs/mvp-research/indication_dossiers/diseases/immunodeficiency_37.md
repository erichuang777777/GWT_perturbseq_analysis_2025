# Immunodeficiency 37

**Platform link**: Target gene **BCL10** (platform signed-DE rank 889, Open Targets genetic-association score 0.86).

## 1. Definition & Population
Immunodeficiency 37 is caused by biallelic loss-of-function mutations in BCL10, a core adaptor of the CARD11-BCL10-MALT1 (CBM) signalosome complex downstream of the T-cell and B-cell antigen receptors. A recent report describes "BCL10 Deficiency Presenting as Severe Combined Immunodeficiency Escaping Newborn Screening," indicating the disease can present with an SCID-like phenotype that is not reliably captured by standard TREC-based newborn screening.

## 2. Epidemiology Highlights
- Not established in this pass — no population-level prevalence/incidence figure for BCL10 deficiency was identified; the condition is documented through individual case reports and mechanistic studies rather than cohort-level epidemiology.
- BCL10 deficiency is grouped with related CARD11 and MALT1 deficiencies under the umbrella term "germline CBM-opathies," described as causing "a broad array of clinical phenotypes, ranging" across immunodeficiency, atopy, and lymphoproliferation — indicating the disease family is recognized as clinically heterogeneous even though granular incidence data are lacking.

## 3. Biology Highlights
- BCL10 forms heterotrimeric complexes with MALT1 and CARD-family adaptors that are essential for NF-κB activation: "Heterotrimers composed of B cell CLL/lymphoma 10 (BCL10), mucosa-associated lymphoid tissue lymphoma translocation protein 1 (MALT1), and caspase recruitment domain-containing (CARD) family adaptors play a role in NF-κB activation" in both innate and adaptive immunity.
- Inherited BCL10 deficiency was shown to impair both "hematopoietic and nonhematopoietic immunity," indicating the defect extends beyond lymphocyte-intrinsic signaling to broader immune competence.
- The CBM complex operates directly downstream of the T-cell receptor, translating TCR engagement into NF-κB-dependent T-cell activation and proliferation — placing BCL10 in the core receptor-proximal signaling node that a CD4+ T-cell perturb-seq platform is designed to interrogate, complementary to CARD11 and MALT1 (the other CBM-opathy genes).

## 4. Current Treatment
No FDA-approved disease-specific pharmacologic therapy for BCL10 deficiency was identified in this pass; management of combined immunodeficiency phenotypes in this gene family is typically supportive and/or via hematopoietic stem cell transplantation per the general combined-immunodeficiency literature, though no BCL10-specific FDA drug application was found in this pass.

## 5. Regulatory & Trials Snapshot
