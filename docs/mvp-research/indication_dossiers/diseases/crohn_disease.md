# Crohn Disease

**Platform link**: Target gene **CYLD** (platform signed-DE rank 36, Open Targets genetic-association score 0.59).

## 1. Definition & Population

Crohn's disease (CD) is a chronic inflammatory intestinal disease, first described at the beginning of the last century, characterized by "the alternation of periods of flares and remissions influenced by a complex pathogenesis in which inflammation plays a key role" (ICD-10 K50). It is one of the two major forms of inflammatory bowel disease (IBD), alongside ulcerative colitis.

## 2. Epidemiology Highlights

- A systematic review of 147 population-based studies found "the prevalence of inflammatory bowel disease exceeded 0·3% in North America, Oceania, and many countries in Europe," with the highest reported Crohn's-specific prevalence at 322 per 100,000 in Germany and 319 per 100,000 in Canada.
- The protease/protease-inhibitor genetic systematic review noted IBD "affects 2.2 million people in Europe and 1.4 million people in North America."

## 3. Biology Highlights

- **CYLD**, the platform's target gene, was identified as the most significant genetic association with Crohn's disease in a genetic association study across five European IBD referral centers (2,320 CD patients): "Ten single nucleotide polymorphisms (SNPs) in four genes were significantly associated with CD: CYLD, USP40, APEH and USP3. CYLD was the most significant gene with the intronically located rs12324931 the strongest associated SNP (p(FDR)=1.74e-17, OR=2.24 (1.83 to 2.74))."
- Mechanistically, CYLD is a deubiquitinase regulating NF-κB signaling; the IBD-associated pathogen adherent-invasive E. coli was shown to "decreas[e] protein levels of the NF-κB regulator CYLD," resulting in "IκB-α degradation and NF-κB activation," directly linking CYLD loss to IBD-relevant inflammatory signaling.
- Broader CD pathophysiology involves innate immunity alterations at the intestinal mucosal barrier and remodeling via metalloproteinases and adhesion molecules.

## 4. Current Treatment

- Biologic therapies (TNF antagonists, anti-integrin, anti-IL-12/23) are standard for moderate-to-severe CD, per a network meta-analysis of Phase 2/3 RCTs.
- Ustekinumab (Stelara), an anti-IL-12/23 biologic, holds FDA approval (BLA125261, original approval 2009-09-25) with a CD-specific supplemental approval on file per Drugs@FDA records.

## 5. Regulatory & Trials Snapshot

ClinicalTrials.gov lists 1,368 interventional trials for Crohn disease, reflecting a mature, heavily studied indication with an active biologics and small-molecule pipeline.
