# Type 1 Diabetes Mellitus

**Platform link**: Target gene **DIPK1A** (platform signed-DE rank 31, Open Targets genetic-association score 0.5).

## 1. Definition & Population
Type 1 diabetes mellitus (T1D) is an autoimmune disease characterized by destruction of insulin-producing pancreatic beta cells, leading to absolute insulin deficiency. It is classically diagnosed in childhood/adolescence but is increasingly recognized in adults, including older adults.

## 2. Epidemiology Highlights
- Global incidence and prevalence patterns are well documented: "Data from large epidemiologic studies worldwide indicate that the incidence of T1D has been increasing by 2% to 5% worldwide and that the prevalence of T1D is approximately 1 in 300 in the United States by 18 years of age."
- T1D is not confined to youth-onset disease: "Although type 1 diabetes mellitus (T1DM) is traditionally viewed as a youth-onset disorder, the number of older adults being diagnosed with this disease is growing." A peak of incidence has been observed in older adults comparable to the classic pediatric peak.

## 3. Biology Highlights
- The classic model attributes disease to autoreactive T cells destroying otherwise healthy beta cells, but recent work proposes an alternative, beta-cell-centric view: "We propose an alternative view in which the &#946;-cell is the key contributor to the disease. By their nature and function, &#946;-cells are prone to biosynthetic stress with limited measures for self-defence." This reframes T1D as arising from an interaction between intrinsic beta-cell stress and the immune attack it provokes, rather than immune dysfunction alone.
- The platform's target gene DIPK1A (also known as FAM69A) belongs to a predicted atypical protein kinase family: "Using bioinformatics tools, we predicted protein kinase structure for proteins coded by five related human genes and their Metazoan homologues, the FAM69 family... An EF-hand Ca(2+)-binding domain in FAM69A" suggests calcium-regulated kinase activity, but this single retrievable record concerns neurological disorders, not T1D or T-cell biology specifically — no direct literature link between DIPK1A/FAM69A and T1D or CD4+ T-cell function was found in this pass (direct search for "DIPK1A type 1 diabetes" returned 0 hits).

## 4. Current Treatment
- Standard-of-care remains lifelong exogenous insulin replacement (multiple daily injections or pump therapy) combined with glucose monitoring; this pass did not re-derive detailed insulin regimen literature (out of scope for a compact pass).
- An immune-modulating disease-modifying therapy is FDA-approved to delay T1D onset in at-risk individuals: teplizumab (brand TZIELD, BLA761183) is an approved CD3-directed monoclonal antibody, confirmed via FDA Drugs@FDA records (application number BLA761183, sponsor Provention Bio, original approval action dated 2022-11-17).

## 5. Regulatory & Trials Snapshot
ClinicalTrials.gov lists a large interventional trial portfolio for type 1 diabetes; a search restricted to interventional studies returned a total of 3,640 matching trials, including completed Phase 2 studies on beta-cell preservation strategies (e.g., NCT04545151, verapamil SR in newly diagnosed T1D).
