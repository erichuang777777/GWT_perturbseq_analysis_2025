# Systemic Lupus Erythematosus

**Platform link**: Target gene **FOXN2** (platform signed-DE rank 1, Open Targets genetic-association score 0.09).

## 1. Definition & Population
Systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE) is a chronic, relapsing-remitting systemic autoimmune disease in which loss of tolerance to nuclear self-antigens drives autoantibody production and immune-complex-mediated damage across multiple organs (skin, joints, kidneys, CNS, hematologic system). It predominantly affects women of childbearing age.

## 2. Epidemiology Highlights
- A global modelling study synthesizing 112 studies estimated worldwide SLE incidence and prevalence with substantial regional variation; the study explicitly set out "to quantify global, regional and country-specific estimates of epidemiology of systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE)" using a Bayesian hierarchical linear mixed model to estimate incidence and prevalence from 112 studies meeting inclusion criteria.
- Belimumab real-world outcomes data come from a pediatric-onset SLE cohort of 182 patients, underscoring that childhood-onset disease is a recognized, actively studied subgroup "This retrospective study included 182 patients with cSLE treated with belimumab".

## 4. Current Treatment
- Belimumab (BENLYSTA), a BLyS/BAFF-inhibiting monoclonal antibody, is FDA-approved for SLE — Drugs@FDA record BLA125370 shows original approval action dated 2011-03-09, with a supplemental BLA (761043) approved 2017-07-20 (subcutaneous formulation).
- "Belimumab is the only biologic approved for childhood-onset systemic lupus erythematosus (cSLE)", per a 2026 real-world retrospective study on treatment timing and duration.
- Standard-of-care background therapy (hydroxychloroquine, corticosteroids, immunosuppressants such as mycophenolate/azathioprine) is well established but not itemized with a specific source in this pass.
