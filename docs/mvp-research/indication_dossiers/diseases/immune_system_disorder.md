# Immune System Disorder

**Platform link**: Target genes **PLA2G4A** (platform signed-DE rank 23, Open Targets genetic-association score 0.35) and **PDIA6** (platform signed-DE rank 32, Open Targets genetic-association score 0.36).

## 1. Definition & Population

## 3. Biology Highlights
- PLA2G4A (cytosolic phospholipase A2, cPLA2α) has documented roles in inflammatory joint disease: in rheumatoid arthritis synoviocytes it "regulates TNF-induced production of joint destructive effectors," linking it to cytokine-driven inflammatory signaling relevant to immune-mediated tissue damage.
- PLA2G4A variants have also been associated with atopic/allergic immune phenotypes, with one study reporting an "association between PLA2G4A and P2RX7 genes and eosinophilic phenotype and environment with pediatric asthma."
- PDIA6 (protein disulfide isomerase family A member 6), an endoplasmic-reticulum protein-folding enzyme, was shown in a forward genetic mouse screen to be essential for immune cell development: mice with a causative Pdia6 mutation displayed a "syndromic disorder marked by growth retardation, diabetes, premature death, and severe lymphoid and myeloid hypoplasia together with diminished T cell-independent (TI) antibody responses," directly establishing PDIA6 as required for normal lymphoid/myeloid development.
