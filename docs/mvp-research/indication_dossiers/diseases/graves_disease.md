# Graves Disease

**Platform link**: Target gene **ITM2A** (platform signed-DE rank 18, Open Targets genetic-association score 0.52).

## 1. Definition & Population

Graves' disease (GD) is "the most common cause of hyperthyroidism in developed Countries", an autoimmune disorder driven by stimulatory TSH-receptor autoantibodies (ICD-10 E05.0). It is "more common between 30 and 60 years; 5-10 times more frequent in women".

## 2. Epidemiology Highlights

- Genetic predisposition accounts for a large majority of GD risk, with environmental factors contributing the remainder: "The genetic predisposition accounts for 79% of the risk for GD, while environmental factors for 21%."
- Graves' orbitopathy, the main extrathyroidal manifestation, "affects about 30% of Graves' patients, although fewer than 10% have severe forms requiring immunosuppressive treatments."

## 3. Biology Highlights

- **ITM2A**, the platform's target gene, is a validated GD susceptibility gene on the X chromosome discovered via genetic/environmental interaction analysis: the study title itself states "ITM2A Expands Evidence for Genetic and Environmental Interaction in Graves Disease Pathogenesis." This X-linked locus has been proposed to help explain the strong female predominance of autoimmune thyroid disease.
- Independent pediatric cohorts replicated the ITM2A association: a Korean pediatric autoimmune thyroid disease study reported "much attention has been directed towards the integral membrane protein 2A (ITM2A) gene located on the X chromosome" in the context of female-predominant autoimmune thyroid disease, and a companion study examined ITM2A alongside GPR174 polymorphisms on the X chromosome in Korean children with autoimmune thyroid disease.
- Disease-driving TSH-receptor antibodies (TSH-R-Abs) are the specific biomarker of GD and directly stimulate thyroid hormone overproduction.

## 4. Current Treatment

- Antithyroid drugs (thionamides), radioactive iodine, and thyroidectomy are the three established treatment pillars for GD, with TSH-receptor antibody titers used to guide management, per a clinical update on GD and Graves orbitopathy management.
- Methimazole is FDA-approved (multiple ANDA generics on file, e.g., ANDA040350/Mylan) as a first-line antithyroid thionamide.

## 5. Regulatory & Trials Snapshot

ClinicalTrials.gov lists 225 interventional trials for Graves disease, spanning antithyroid drug comparisons, orbitopathy immunosuppressive trials, and biomarker studies (e.g., NCT06451016, a completed vitamin D supplementation trial in GD).
