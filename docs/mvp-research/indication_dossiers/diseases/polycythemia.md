# Polycythemia (Polycythemia Vera)

**Platform link**: Target gene **THAP5** (platform signed-DE rank 7, Open Targets genetic-association score 0.34).

## 1. Definition & Population
Polycythemia vera (PV) is a chronic myeloproliferative neoplasm (MPN) characterized by clonal overproduction of red blood cells (often with concurrent leukocytosis/thrombocytosis), driven in >95% of cases by the JAK2 V617F mutation. It carries an elevated thrombotic risk and typically presents in adults over 60.

## 2. Epidemiology Highlights
- A Swedish nationwide population-based registry study analyzed 2,604 PV patients (alongside 3,141 essential thrombocythemia patients) across 43,612 patient-years, giving a large real-world epidemiologic and outcomes base "In this nationwide, population-based study, we analyzed 2604 PV and 3141 ET patients using multiple Swedish health care registers, covering 43,612 patient-years".
- A US SEER-based analysis of MPN incidence trends from 1973–2011 found that the 2005 JAK2 V617F discovery and 2008 WHO diagnostic guideline update coincided with a measurable shift in subtype-specific incidence rates "The 2005 JAK2 V617F discovery and the 2008 WHO diagnostic guideline for the JAK2 V617F mutation coincide with a 31% increase in ET and a 21% decrease in PV incidence rates".

## 3. Biology Highlights
- THAP5 returned zero direct PubMed hits for "THAP5 polycythemia" — not established in this pass, despite its OT genetic-association score of 0.34.
- The dominant PV pathogenic mechanism is well characterized: JAK2 V617F is a shared driver mutation across PV, essential thrombocythemia, and primary myelofibrosis, producing constitutive JAK-STAT activation — "a shared codon 617 substitution of JAK2...leads to a constitutively active tyrosine kinase and a pathogenic lesion that unites all three polycythemia vera, essential thrombocythemia, and primary myelofibrosis", with this mutation "interfer[ing] with the autoinhibitory regulation and result[ing] in persistent downstream JAK-STAT signaling".
- This JAK-STAT-centric biology is a myeloid/megakaryocyte-progenitor mechanism rather than a CD4+ T-cell-intrinsic pathway, so the platform's T-cell perturb-seq rationale for THAP5 in PV is not established by literature in this pass.

## 4. Current Treatment
- Ruxolitinib (JAKAFI), an oral JAK1/2 inhibitor, is FDA-approved and used in PV patients with inadequate response to hydroxyurea — Drugs@FDA NDA202192 shows original approval action dated 2011-11-16; the sponsor (Incyte) has since expanded the franchise (topical OPZELURA 2021; extended-release JAKAFI XR 2026).
- Real-world German claims-data analysis of PV patients tracked "thrombotic outcomes and treatment patterns...phlebotomy frequency, and the development of hydroxyurea (HU) intolerance or resistance", indicating phlebotomy and hydroxyurea remain first-line management alongside JAK inhibition for refractory/intolerant cases.
