# Psoriatic Arthritis

**Platform link**: Target gene **KIF16B** (platform signed-DE rank 46, Open Targets genetic-association score 0.47).

## 1. Definition & Population
Psoriatic arthritis (PsA) is a chronic inflammatory arthritis that develops in a subset of patients with psoriasis, involving peripheral joints, entheses, the spine, and skin/nails. It is classified as a spondyloarthritis and is an immune-mediated, HLA-associated disease affecting both sexes, typically presenting years after cutaneous psoriasis onset.

## 3. Biology Highlights
- PsA pathogenesis is closely tied to the IL-23/IL-17 axis: "This article reviews the immunologic role of interleukin (IL)-17, the major effector cytokine in the pathogenesis of psoriatic disease, along with the rationale for targeting the IL-17 cytokine family (IL-17A, IL-17F, and IL-17 receptor A) in the treatment of psoriasis and psoriatic arthritis."
- The platform's target gene KIF16B encodes a kinesin motor protein rather than a classical cytokine/immune signaling node: "As a member of the kinesin-3 family, kinesin family member 16B (KIF16B) has a characteristic PhoX homology (PX) domain that binds to membranes containing phosphatidylinositol-3-phosphate (PI(3)P) and moves along microtubule filaments to the plus end via a process regulated by coiled coils in the stalk region in various cell types." Its established function is intracellular endosomal cargo transport: "KIF16B is a newly identified kinesin that regulates the intracellular motility of early endosomes."
- No direct literature link between KIF16B and psoriatic arthritis or T-cell immune biology was found in this pass (a direct PubMed search for "KIF16B psoriatic arthritis" returned 0 hits) — the connection to disease is inferred only from the platform's perturb-seq signed-DE ranking and the Open Targets genetic-association score, not from an independent mechanistic study.

## 4. Current Treatment
- JAK inhibitors (oral small molecules) and IL-17/IL-23 biologics are part of the standard treatment armamentarium for psoriatic disease; regarding JAK inhibitor safety in this class: "Janus kinase (JAK) inhibitors are disease-modifying agents with efficacy in treating a spectrum of burdensome dermatologic conditions. The US Food and Drug Administration (FDA) recently placed a black box warning on this class of medications due to safety concerns based on data from studies investigating tofacitinib in patients with rheumatoid arthritis."
- A case report describes combination use of a TYK2 inhibitor (deucravacitinib) in a patient with coexisting psoriatic and rheumatoid arthritis: "Successful Management of Multidrug-Resistant Psoriatic Arthritis Coexisting With Rheumatoid Arthritis Using a TYK2 Inhibitor Deucravacitinib-Based Regimen." FDA records confirm deucravacitinib (brand SOTYKTU, NDA214958) is an approved oral TYK2 inhibitor, though its labeled indication is plaque psoriasis, not PsA specifically — this pass did not confirm a PsA-specific FDA approval for deucravacitinib.
- No FDA-approved therapy specifically targeting KIF16B was identified in this pass (expected, as it is a platform-nominated target, not a clinical drug target).
