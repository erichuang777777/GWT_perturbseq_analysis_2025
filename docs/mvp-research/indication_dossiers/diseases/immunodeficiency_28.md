# Immunodeficiency 28

**Platform link**: Target gene **IFNGR2** (platform signed-DE rank 33, Open Targets genetic-association score 0.9).

## 1. Definition & Population
Immunodeficiency 28 is caused by biallelic mutations in IFNGR2, encoding the interferon-gamma receptor 2 subunit, and falls within the group of disorders called Mendelian Susceptibility to Mycobacterial Disease (MSMD). Complete and partial forms exist depending on residual receptor function, with patients presenting with selective susceptibility to weakly virulent mycobacteria (including BCG vaccine strains and environmental mycobacteria) in otherwise healthy individuals.

## 2. Epidemiology Highlights
- MSMD overall is described as "a rare genetic disorder characterized by impaired immunity against intracellular pathogens," documented through national retrospective cohorts (e.g., 32 Iranian patients, a Mexican cohort) rather than a single global prevalence estimate; no IFNGR2-specific incidence figure was found in this pass.
- MSMD as a genetic category comprises "16 genes associated with MSMD" accounting for "31 distinct disorders with variable clinical presentations," of which IFNGR2 biallelic deficiency is one recognized cause.

## 3. Biology Highlights
- IFNGR2 loss-of-function disrupts IFN-gamma receptor signaling; "all known genetic etiologies disrupt interferon (IFN)-gamma immunity," and "germline bi-allelic mutations of IFNGR2 can underlie partial or complete forms" of MSMD, establishing a direct genotype-phenotype gradient based on residual receptor activity.
- A dominant-negative IFNGR2 mutation causing partial disease was characterized biochemically, showing "no IFN-gammaR activity" in homozygotes with "only partial" loss in heterozygous carriers, demonstrating the IFN-gamma receptor pathway's dosage sensitivity.
- The IFN-gamma responsive pathway has been studied using patient iPSC-derived macrophages to model "inborn errors of the IFN-gamma responsive pathway," underscoring that macrophage/innate-immune IFN-gamma signaling (downstream of the CD4+ T-cell-derived IFN-gamma that the platform's T-cell perturb-seq system measures) is the operative axis in this disease.

## 4. Current Treatment
Recombinant interferon gamma-1b (brand name ACTIMMUNE) is an FDA-approved product from Horizon Therapeutics Ireland DAC; its FDA label indications are "reducing the frequency and severity of serious infections associated with Chronic Granulomatous Disease (CGD)" and "delaying time to disease progression in patients with severe, malignant osteopetrosis (SMO)" — not IFNGR2 deficiency/MSMD specifically. No disease-specific (IFNGR2-deficiency/MSMD) FDA-approved indication was identified in this pass.

## 5. Regulatory & Trials Snapshot
A ClinicalTrials.gov search for "Mendelian susceptibility to mycobacterial disease" returned only 2 total trial records, reflecting the rarity and limited dedicated interventional trial infrastructure for this gene-defined disease group.
