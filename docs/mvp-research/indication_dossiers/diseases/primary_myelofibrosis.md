# Primary Myelofibrosis

**Platform link**: Target gene **CALR** (platform signed-DE rank 17, Open Targets genetic-association score 0.72 — the highest OT score in this batch).

## 1. Definition & Population
Primary myelofibrosis (PMF) is a clonal myeloproliferative neoplasm (MPN) of hematopoietic stem/progenitor cells characterized by bone marrow fibrosis and abnormal hematopoiesis, driven by JAK-STAT pathway activation via one of three canonical driver mutations (JAK2, CALR, or MPL). It can arise de novo or evolve from prior PV/ET, and typically affects older adults.

## 2. Epidemiology Highlights
- A Swedish nationwide population-based study identified 1,079 patients with myelofibrosis matched against 5,395 controls, providing large-cohort epidemiologic and outcomes data "In this nationwide population-based study, we assessed the frequency of major bleeding, thrombotic events, and all-cause mortality in 1079 patients diagnosed with MF and 5395 matched controls using multiple Swedish health care registers".
- A Canadian (Ontario) population-based registry captured all incident MPN cases 2004–2019, including 1,385 primary myelofibrosis cases among 10,336 total MPN cases "all incident cases of MPN from the Ontario cancer registry between 2004 and 2019 (N=10,336; ET=5108; PV=3843; MF=1385)".

## 3. Biology Highlights
- CALR is one of the three canonical MPN driver mutations: a 2026 review states MPN patients "shar[e] a small set of canonical driver mutations in JAK2, CALR, or MPL" yet exhibit heterogeneous clinical trajectories toward myelofibrosis.
- Downstream mechanism converges on JAK-STAT: PMF is "characterized by abnormal proliferation of hematopoiesis, associated with pathologically increased fibrosis in the bone marrow, which is primarily caused by activation of the JAK2 signaling pathway" — mutant CALR is understood in the field to activate the thrombopoietin receptor (MPL)/JAK-STAT axis independent of JAK2 mutation status, though this specific mechanistic step was not directly quoted from a source retrieved in this pass.
- As with the other MPNs in this batch, CALR's role is centered on the myeloid/megakaryocyte progenitor compartment rather than CD4+ T cells; a direct literature tie to the platform's CD4+ T-cell perturb-seq context was not identified in this pass.

## 4. Current Treatment
- Fedratinib (INREBIC), an oral JAK2 inhibitor, is FDA-approved for intermediate/high-risk myelofibrosis — Drugs@FDA NDA212327 shows original approval action dated 2019-08-16 (sponsor Bristol-Myers Squibb).
- Ruxolitinib (JAKAFI) is also widely used in myelofibrosis (see polycythemia dossier for its FDA record), and the German ONKOPEDIA 2025 guideline for PMF frames treatment around JAK2 pathway activation as the central fibrotic driver.
