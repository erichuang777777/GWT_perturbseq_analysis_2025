# Ulcerative Colitis

**Platform link**: Target gene **ERN1** (platform signed-DE rank 21, Open Targets genetic-association score 0.45).

## 1. Definition & Population
Ulcerative colitis (UC) is a chronic relapsing-remitting inflammatory bowel disease affecting the colonic mucosa in a continuous distribution starting from the rectum, causing bloody diarrhea, urgency, and abdominal pain. It typically has a bimodal age of onset (young adulthood and a smaller peak in later life).

## 2. Epidemiology Highlights
- Global incidence and prevalence of inflammatory bowel disease (which encompasses UC and Crohn's disease) have been systematically reviewed: "Inflammatory bowel disease is a global disease in the 21st century. We aimed to assess the changing incidence and prevalence of inflammatory bowel disease around the world." The review searched population-based studies from 1990 onward covering both ulcerative colitis and Crohn's disease incidence/prevalence trends.
- A specific numeric UC-only prevalence figure with verbatim quote was not established in this pass; the systematic review abstract retrieved describes methodology rather than a single top-line number.

## 3. Biology Highlights
- ERN1 encodes IRE1 (inositol-requiring enzyme 1), a key sensor of the unfolded protein response (UPR) implicated in UC intestinal epithelial pathology: "Ulcerative colitis (UC) is characterized by a chronic overproduction of proinflammatory cytokines. During an acute phase, the endoplasmic reticulum (ER) is overloaded and the protein folding process is impaired, a condition named ER stress. This state induces a response (unfolded protein response (UPR)), initiated by the activation of IRE1/Xbp-1, PERK/eIF2, and ATF6 pathways, which has previously been linked to intestinal inflammation in experimental models."
- Beyond epithelial cells, IRE1&#945;-XBP1 (ERN1-driven) signaling directly controls T-cell mitochondrial activity and effector function in other inflammatory/tumor contexts: "Here we report that ovarian cancer...induces endoplasmic reticulum stress and activates the IRE1&#945;-XBP1 arm of the unfolded protein response in T cells to control their mitochondrial respiration and anti-tumour function." This supports a plausible, though not UC-specific, mechanistic link between ERN1/IRE1 signaling and CD4+ T-cell function relevant to the perturb-seq platform's biology, though a UC-specific T-cell IRE1 study was not retrieved in this pass.
- Therapeutic modulation of the IRE1/XBP1 axis in UC has been studied preclinically, e.g., mesenchymal stem cell therapy alleviating UC epithelial apoptosis via this pathway: "Human umbilical cord mesenchymal stem cells alleviate colon epithelial apoptosis in ulcerative colitis by activating inositol-requiring enzyme 1/X-box binding protein 1 signaling."

## 4. Current Treatment
Standard UC therapy includes 5-aminosalicylates, corticosteroids, immunomodulators (thiopurines), biologics (anti-TNF, anti-integrin, anti-IL-12/23), and JAK inhibitors. No FDA-approved therapy specifically targeting ERN1/IRE1 was identified in this pass — Not established in this pass.

## 5. Regulatory & Trials Snapshot
ClinicalTrials.gov lists a substantial interventional trial base for ulcerative colitis; a search restricted to interventional studies returned a total of 1,290 matching trials. A relevant example is a completed Phase 2b dose-ranging study of oral JAK inhibitors PF-06651600 and PF-06700841 as induction and chronic therapy in moderate-to-severe UC (NCT02958865).
