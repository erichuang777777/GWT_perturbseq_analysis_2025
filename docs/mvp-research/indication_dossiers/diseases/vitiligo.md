# Vitiligo

**Platform link**: Target gene **TMEM131L** (platform signed-DE rank 5, Open Targets genetic-association score 0.43).

## 1. Definition & Population
Vitiligo is a chronic autoimmune depigmenting skin disorder characterized by selective destruction of melanocytes, producing well-demarcated, chalky-white cutaneous macules. It affects both sexes and can present at any age.

## 2. Epidemiology Highlights
- Global prevalence is well characterized: "Vitiligo, a common depigmenting skin disorder, has an estimated prevalence of 0.5-2% of the population worldwide."
- The disease is formally classified within the autoimmune disease spectrum: "considerable progress has been made in our understanding of the pathogenesis of vitiligo which is now clearly classified as an autoimmune disease." An international consensus classification was established in 2011 per the same review.

## 3. Biology Highlights
- CD8+ T cells and dendritic cells are central effector/orchestrator populations that destroy melanocytes: "Although dendritic cell (DC)- and CD8T cell-mediated autoimmunity is critical for destroying melanocytes in vitiligo, treatment options remain limited by the absence of therapies that cotarget both cell types." A 2025 study found the immunoregulatory metabolite itaconate modulates this axis and tested a therapeutic derivative (dimethyl itaconate) in mouse models.
- Keratinocyte-derived IL-15 activates memory CD8+ T cells via JAK-STAT signaling as a proposed mechanistic link between oxidative stress and T-cell-mediated melanocyte killing: "Oxidative stress and effector memory CD8T cells have been greatly implicated in vitiligo pathogenesis...epidermal IL-15 expression was mainly derived from keratinocytes" and its expression correlated with epidermal hydrogen peroxide content, driving CD8+ T-cell activation via the JAK-STAT pathway.
- The platform's target gene TMEM131L was identified as a regulator of T-cell development and Wnt signaling in the thymus: "we identify transmembrane protein 131-like (TMEM131L) as a novel regulator of thymocyte proliferation and demonstrate that it corresponds to a not as yet reported inhibitor of Wnt signaling." Silencing TMEM131L in human hematopoietic progenitors caused thymocyte hyperproliferation and developmental defects consistent with deregulated Wnt signaling, per the same study. A related study also shows TMEM131L interferes with Notch/Wnt crosstalk in a heterologous model: "TMEM131L interference with the Wnt pathway results primarily from a Notch-dependent decrease in Wingless production." No direct literature link between TMEM131L and vitiligo specifically was found in this pass (direct search for "TMEM131L vitiligo" returned 0 hits) — the disease connection is inferred from the platform's CD4+ T-cell perturb-seq ranking and Open Targets score, building on TMEM131L's independently established role in T-cell/thymocyte development.

## 4. Current Treatment
Topical and systemic JAK inhibitors have become a standard-of-care option for repigmentation. FDA records confirm ruxolitinib cream (brand OPZELURA, NDA215309) as an approved topical JAK1/JAK2 inhibitor product, distinct from the oral JAKAFI (NDA202192) formulation used in myeloproliferative disease — Opzelura's vitiligo indication reflects the JAK-STAT-driven CD8+ T-cell mechanism described above, though this pass did not independently re-confirm the vitiligo indication text from the FDA label.

## 5. Regulatory & Trials Snapshot
ClinicalTrials.gov lists an active interventional trial base for vitiligo; a search restricted to interventional studies returned a total of 261 matching trials, including an ongoing Phase 1 trial of mesenchymal stromal cell-derived extracellular vesicles (NCT06813027, active/not recruiting) and a completed Phase 2 trial of tofacitinib for immune skin conditions in Down syndrome (NCT04246372).
