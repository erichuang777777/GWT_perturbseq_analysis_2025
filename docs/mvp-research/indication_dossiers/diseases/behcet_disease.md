# Behçet Disease

**Platform link**: Target gene **NPHP4** (platform signed-DE rank 39, Open Targets genetic-association score 0.45).

## 1. Definition & Population

Behçet disease (BD) is an inflammatory multisystem disorder of unknown etiology, believed to be triggered by infection and environmental factors in genetically predisposed individuals, presenting with recurrent oral/genital ulcers, skin lesions, and uveitis, with occasional vascular, gastrointestinal, and neurologic involvement (ICD-10 M35.2).

## 2. Epidemiology Highlights

- Global prevalence is estimated at 10.3 per 100,000 population, with the highest rates historically observed along the Silk Route (Turkey, Middle East, East Asia) — "prevalence estimated at 10.3 (95% CI, 6.1, 17.7) per 100,000 population, with higher rates observed in regions historically linked to the Silk Route."
- Intestinal involvement, a severe subtype, is more prevalent in East Asian than Mediterranean BD populations, per a large Korean/Turkish/Japanese GWAS cohort.

## 3. Biology Highlights

- A GWAS of 1,689 Korean and Turkish BD patients (replicated in 593 Japanese patients) identified **NPHP4** as a novel genome-wide-significant susceptibility locus for overall BD, alongside TYW1-AUTS2 and SEMA6D — "We identified three novel genome-wide significant susceptibility loci including NPHP4" (rs74566205), TYW1-AUTS2, and SEMA6D for overall BD (exact p-value exponents were rendered blank/truncated in the source abstract text as returned by PubMed and are omitted here rather than guessed) This is a direct genetic link supporting the platform's NPHP4 signal, though the paper does not report a specific immune-cell mechanism for NPHP4 in T cells.
- BD immunopathogenesis broadly involves disrupted Th1/Th2 and Th17/Treg balance and B-cell activation, best characterized in the neuro-Behçet subtype where "increased IL-17, IL-10, T-bet/GATA-3, and retinoic acid related orphan receptor (ROR)-γt/Foxp3 ratios, marking the disrupted scale of the Th1/Th2 and Th17/Treg axis" were reported.
- A distinct HLA-B*46:01 association was found specific to intestinal BD, separate from the classic HLA-B*51:01 risk allele.

## 4. Current Treatment

- TNF-alpha inhibitors are the pivotal biologic class for BD, with newer agents including IL-1 inhibitors and ustekinumab broadening options — "treatment strategies, highlighting TNF-alpha inhibitors as pivotal biologics and newer agents like IL-1 inhibitors and Ustekinumab that broaden the therapeutic options for BD."
- Apremilast (Otezla), an oral PDE4 inhibitor approved by FDA under NDA205437 (original approval 2014-03-21, per Drugs@FDA), is used off-label/adjacent for BD oral ulcers: "Apremilast, an oral phosphodiesterase 4 inhibitor, is used to treat psoriasis... current evidence shows that apremilast is effective for oral ulcer, the most common symptom of Behçet's disease."

## 5. Regulatory & Trials Snapshot

ClinicalTrials.gov lists 70 interventional trials for Behçet disease. A notable ongoing Phase 2 trial (NCT06849908, Peking Union Medical College Hospital, recruiting) is comparing baricitinib vs. adalimumab in refractory intestinal Behçet's syndrome.
