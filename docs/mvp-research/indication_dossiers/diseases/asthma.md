# Asthma

**Platform link**: Target gene **SIK2** (platform signed-DE rank 3, Open Targets genetic-association score 0.72).

## 1. Definition & Population

Asthma is a common chronic inflammatory disease of the airways (ICD-10 J45), affecting individuals across all ages, characterized in most patients by chronic type 2 (Th2-driven) airway inflammation, with a subset presenting as severe, therapy-resistant disease.

## 2. Epidemiology Highlights

- Per the GBD 2021 study, "In 2021, there were an estimated 260 million (95% UI 227-298) individuals with asthma... worldwide", with cases having declined from 287 million in 1990 to 238 million in 2005 before rising again to 260 million in 2021.
- Despite rising absolute case counts, "age-standardised prevalence rates decreased-by 40·0% (from 5568·3 per 100,000 to 3340·1 per 100,000) for asthma" between 1990 and 2021, and modifiable risk factors (high BMI, occupational asthmagens, smoking, NO2 pollution) accounted for "29·9% of the global asthma DALY burden."

## 3. Biology Highlights

- Airway inflammation in most asthma patients is driven by chronic type 2 immunity: "CD4 T helper 2 (Th2) cells are the canonical producers of the cytokines that fuel type 2 inflammation: interleukin (IL)-4, IL-5, IL-9, and IL-13." More recently, additional lymphocyte subsets beyond canonical Th2 cells (group 2 innate lymphoid cells, type 2 CD8 cytotoxic T cells) have been implicated in driving type 2 inflammation, particularly in therapy-resistant severe asthma.
- **No PubMed literature specifically linking SIK2 (salt-inducible kinase 2) to asthma or CD4+ T-cell asthma biology was found in this pass**; a direct query for "SIK2 asthma T cell" returned zero results, and a broader immune-function query for SIK2 also returned no hits. This is a gap: the platform's high genetic-association score (0.72) for SIK2 in asthma is not independently corroborated by the PubMed search performed here.

## 4. Current Treatment

- Standard-of-care spans inhaled corticosteroids/bronchodilators for most patients, escalating to targeted biologics for severe type-2-high asthma. Dupilumab (anti-IL-4Rα, blocking both IL-4 and IL-13 signaling) is FDA-approved (BLA761055, original approval 2017-03-28) and has an asthma indication among its approved uses.
- A recent head-to-head Phase 4 trial (EVEREST) directly compared dupilumab and omalizumab in patients with coexisting asthma and chronic rhinosinusitis with nasal polyps, reflecting the maturity of the type-2-biologics treatment landscape: "the biologics dupilumab and omalizumab, which target drivers and mediators of type 2 inflammation (interleukin [IL]-4/IL-13 signaling and immunoglobulin E [IgE], respectively), are efficacious in treating CRSwNP."

## 5. Regulatory & Trials Snapshot

ClinicalTrials.gov lists 3,771 interventional trials for asthma, reflecting one of the most heavily studied indications in this batch, with an active and diversified biologics pipeline (anti-IL-4/13, anti-IL-5, anti-TSLP) beyond the classical inhaled corticosteroid/bronchodilator standard of care.
