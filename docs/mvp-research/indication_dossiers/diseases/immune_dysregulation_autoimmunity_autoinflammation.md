# Immune Dysregulation, Autoimmunity, and Autoinflammation

**Platform link**: Target gene **PLCG1** (platform signed-DE rank 520, Open Targets genetic-association score 0.55).

## 1. Definition & Population
This label corresponds most directly to a recently described monogenic disease caused by a germline gain-of-function variant in PLCG1 (phospholipase C gamma 1), reported as a new immune dysregulation disorder rather than a long-established named syndrome. It sits alongside a broader family of "CBM/PLC-opathy" disorders (e.g., PLCG2-associated PLAID/APLAID) that share the immune dysregulation/autoinflammation phenotype pattern but map to paralog genes.

## 2. Epidemiology Highlights
- No population prevalence/incidence figure was found in this pass; the PLCG1 germline variant disease was described as a single new case/family report, consistent with an ultra-rare, recently characterized monogenic condition. "Not established in this pass."
- The related PLCG2-associated disorders (PLAID/APLAID) are themselves described as comprising "broad and distinct clinical presentations related to functional classes of genetic variants," indicating this gene family produces a phenotypic spectrum rather than a single uniform disease, but PLCG2 is a paralog, not the platform's PLCG1 target itself.

## 3. Biology Highlights
- PLCG1 is described as "a critical enzyme regulating nuclear factor-κB (NF-κB), extracellular signal-related kinase, mitogen-activated protein kinase, and nuclear factor of activated T cells signaling pathways," and until the cited 2023 report, "germline PLCG1 mutation in human disease has not been reported."
- The causal mechanism identified was "a gain-of-function variation in PLCG1 [that] causes a new immune dysregulation disease," directly implicating PLCG1 hyperactivation (rather than loss-of-function) in driving the phenotype — relevant context for a CD4+ T-cell perturb-seq platform since PLCG1 sits downstream of the T-cell receptor and mediates NFAT/NF-κB/MAPK activation in T lymphocytes.
- Somatic PLCG1 mutations (distinct from the germline disease-causing variant) also recur in T-cell lymphomas such as angioimmunoblastic T-cell lymphoma, underscoring the gene's general role in dysregulated T-cell activation and proliferation.

## 4. Current Treatment
No FDA-approved disease-specific therapy for the PLCG1 germline immune dysregulation disease was identified in this pass. "No FDA-approved disease-specific therapy identified in this pass."

## 5. Regulatory & Trials Snapshot
A ClinicalTrials.gov search using the broader condition term "immune dysregulation" returned 65 total interventional/observational trial records; a search restricted to the gene term "PLCG1" returned zero trials, consistent with this being a very recently described, single-report monogenic condition without a dedicated trial program.
