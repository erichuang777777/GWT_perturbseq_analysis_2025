# Primary Thrombocytopenia (Primary Immune Thrombocytopenia, ITP)

**Platform link**: Target gene **TMEM131** (platform signed-DE rank 34, Open Targets genetic-association score 0.39).

**Note on disease-label interpretation**: "Primary thrombocytopenia" was interpreted as primary immune thrombocytopenia (ITP) — the current official nomenclature for what was historically called idiopathic thrombocytopenic purpura — rather than essential thrombocythemia (a distinct MPN with elevated, not reduced, platelet counts). Both interpretations were searched; findings below center on ITP.

## 1. Definition & Population
Primary immune thrombocytopenia (ITP) is an acquired autoimmune disorder characterized by isolated low platelet counts due to antibody-mediated platelet destruction and impaired platelet production, in the absence of an identifiable secondary cause. It affects both children (often post-viral, self-limited) and adults (more often chronic), with disease duration classified as acute (<3 months), persistent (3–12 months), or chronic (>12 months).

## 2. Epidemiology Highlights
- A narrative review reports ITP incidence rates directly: "Immune thrombocytopenia (ITP) occurs with an incidence rate of 1.6 to 3.9 per 100,000 patient-years, which increases with age and has a slight female preponderance".
- The same source clarifies the standard disease-duration classification used clinically and in trials: "ITP is termed acute, persistent or chronic when its duration is <3 months, 3 to 12 months and >12 months, respectively".

## 3. Biology Highlights
- TMEM131 returned zero direct PubMed hits for "TMEM131 thrombocytopenia" — not established in this pass, despite its OT genetic-association score of 0.39.
- ITP pathophysiology centers on autoantibody-mediated platelet clearance and increased platelet apoptosis; a mechanistic study of severe ITP patients used platelet apoptosis assays for diagnostic insight, noting the diagnostic challenge posed by very low platelet counts: "Given the low platelet counts in patients with immune thrombocytopenia (< 100 ×10/L) and its pathophysiological complexity, it remains both a diagnostic and therapeutic challenge".
- As an antibody-mediated autoimmune disease, ITP pathogenesis involves autoreactive B cells and CD4+ T-cell help, consistent with the general rationale for including it in a CD4+ T-cell perturb-seq target-discovery platform, though no source in this pass specifically connects TMEM131 to that CD4+ T-cell mechanism.

## 4. Current Treatment
- Thrombopoietin receptor agonists (TPO-RAs) are established second-line therapy; eltrombopag is FDA-approved (11 Drugs@FDA records located, reflecting multiple generic ANDA approvals for eltrombopag olamine alongside the branded product).
- A pediatric pilot study of oral TPO-RAs (predominantly hetrombopag, a newer agent) in refractory/relapsed ITP notes: "thrombopoietin receptor agonists (TPO-RAs) are effective second-line treatments for pediatric immune thrombocytopenia (ITP), real-world data on newer agents like hetrombopag remain scarce".
