# Spondyloenchondrodysplasia with Immune Dysregulation

**Platform link**: Target gene **ZNF627** (platform signed-DE rank 15, Open Targets genetic-association score 0.94).

## 1. Definition & Population

Spondyloenchondrodysplasia with immune dysregulation (SPENCDI, OMIM 607944) is "an uncommon immune-skeletal dysplasia with heterogeneous manifestations and variable severity", characterized by spondylar and metaphyseal skeletal lesions, immune dysfunction, and neurological involvement. It is "a rare autosomal recessive genetic disorder caused by a homozygous mutation of the ACP5 gene", presenting primarily in childhood.

## 3. Biology Highlights

- The disease's established monogenic cause is biallelic loss-of-function mutation in **ACP5** (encoding tartrate-resistant acid phosphatase), not the platform's ZNF627 target: "a potential diagnosis of spondyloenchondrodysplasia (SPENCD), which was confirmed with the identification of biallelic ACP5 mutations." ACP5 loss drives excess intracellular type I interferon signaling (an interferonopathy).
- Clinical immune manifestations include autoimmune cytopenias (Coombs-positive hemolytic anemia, thrombocytopenia) and can mimic monogenic lupus: "Large spectrum of immunologic abnormalities may be seen in SPENCD, including immune deficiencies and autoimmune disorders."
