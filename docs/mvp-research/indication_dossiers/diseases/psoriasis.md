# Psoriasis

**Platform link**: Target gene **KLHDC10** (platform signed-DE rank 37, Open Targets genetic-association score 0.4).

## 1. Definition & Population
Psoriasis is a chronic, immune-mediated inflammatory skin disease characterized by well-demarcated, scaly erythematous plaques, driven by dysregulated innate and adaptive immunity centered on the IL-23/Th17/IL-17 axis. It affects both sexes across all ages, with a bimodal age-of-onset distribution.

## 2. Epidemiology Highlights
- A global systematic analysis and modelling study of psoriasis epidemiology synthesized incidence and prevalence data worldwide; the study's stated aim was "To systematically review and provide information on the incidence of psoriasis and quantify global, regional, and country specific estimates of its prevalence", drawing on multiple international databases including Medline, Embase, and regional registries.

## 3. Biology Highlights
- KLHDC10 returned zero direct PubMed hits for "KLHDC10 psoriasis" — not established in this pass, despite its OT genetic-association score of 0.4.
- The IL-23/Th17/IL-17 axis is the well-established central pathogenic pathway: "Plaque psoriasis (PP) is a chronic immune-mediated inflammatory disease driven by dysregulation of the IL-23/Th17/IL-17 axis", and this same study performed longitudinal immune profiling of PD-1 engagement and circulating Th17 effector activity during IL-17A-targeted biologic treatment, directly implicating CD4+ Th17 cell dynamics in disease control.
- A 2026 review further frames psoriasis within a broader family of IL-23/Th17-axis immune-mediated diseases, noting shared "inflammatory architecture" with Crohn's disease at the genetic, transcriptomic, and therapeutic level, reinforcing that psoriasis's CD4+ T-cell-centered biology is directly relevant to the platform's discovery rationale, even though KLHDC10 itself is not yet literature-validated in this context.

## 4. Current Treatment
- Secukinumab (COSENTYX), an anti-IL-17A monoclonal antibody, is FDA-approved for psoriasis — Drugs@FDA BLA125504 shows original approval action dated 2015-01-21, with a follow-on BLA (761349) approved 2023-10-06.
- IL-17A-targeted biologics such as secukinumab achieve strong clinical efficacy, and post-treatment immune profiling studies are being used to characterize the systemic immune remodeling that accompanies successful therapy, per the PD-1/Th17 study cited above.
