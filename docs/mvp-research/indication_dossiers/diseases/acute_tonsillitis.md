# Acute Tonsillitis

**Platform link**: Target gene **MAN2A1** (platform signed-DE rank 25, Open Targets genetic-association score 0.31).

## 1. Definition & Population

Acute tonsillitis is an acute inflammatory infection of the palatine tonsils, typically classified together with pharyngitis under "sore throat" (ICD-10 J03), most commonly viral but with a substantial group A beta-hemolytic streptococcal (GABHS) bacterial component. It is part of the broader upper respiratory tract infection (URTI) spectrum affecting all ages, with a pediatric predominance.

## 2. Epidemiology Highlights

- URTIs, including tonsillitis, are a major driver of outpatient care: "Upper respiratory tract infections including nasopharyngitis, pharyngitis, tonsillitis and otitis media constitute 87.5% of the total episodes of respiratory infections."
- "The vast majority of acute upper respiratory tract infections are caused by viruses," underscoring that antibiotic use should be reserved for bacterial (chiefly GABHS) cases.

## 3. Biology Highlights

- Beyond GABHS, anaerobic bacteria are implicated in the acute inflammatory process of tonsillitis, with some species showing interference capability against GABHS: "Some anaerobic bacteria possess interfering capability with Group A beta-hemolytic streptococci (GABHS) and other pathogens," supporting a polymicrobial pathogenesis model.
- **MAN2A1** (alpha-mannosidase II), the platform's target gene, has no tonsillitis-specific literature identified in this pass, but has documented roles in modulating immune/inflammatory responses in adjacent contexts: intestinal-epithelial-specific MAN2A1 deletion ameliorated experimental colitis, and MAN2A1 inhibition enhanced anti-PD-L1 tumor immune responses via altered glycosylation — "Inhibition of MAN2A1 Enhances the Immune Response to Anti-PD-L1 in Human Tumors." These findings support a general immunomodulatory role for MAN2A1-driven glycosylation but do not establish a tonsillitis- or CD4+ T-cell-specific mechanism.

## 4. Current Treatment

- Management centers on differentiating viral from GABHS-driven disease; the German national clinical practice guideline for sore throat provides evidence-based, consensus recommendations for antibiotic use, analgesia, and referral criteria — "The updated German clinical practice guideline on sore throat provides evidence-based recommendations for treatment in the German healthcare system."
- No FDA-approved disease-specific (as opposed to broad-spectrum antibiotic) therapy identified in this pass; treatment is symptomatic care plus penicillin/amoxicillin for confirmed GABHS.

## 5. Regulatory & Trials Snapshot

ClinicalTrials.gov lists 20 interventional trials for acute tonsillitis, generally small trials of antibiotic regimens or adjunctive therapies (e.g., a completed Phase 3 trial of azithromycin sustained-release for tonsillitis/pharyngitis/sinusitis in Japan, NCT00393835) rather than a large modern biologics pipeline.
