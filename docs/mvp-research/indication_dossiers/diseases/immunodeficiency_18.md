# Immunodeficiency 18

**Platform link**: Target gene **CD3E** (platform signed-DE rank 401, Open Targets genetic-association score 0.92).

## 1. Definition & Population
Immunodeficiency 18 is caused by biallelic mutations in CD3E, encoding the CD3-epsilon subunit of the T-cell receptor (TCR) complex, and belongs to the family of CD3-subunit T-cell immunodeficiencies. Homozygous mutations in CD3-chain genes lead to a block in T-cell development, producing a severe combined immunodeficiency (SCID)-like or profound T-cell-deficient phenotype presenting in infancy.

- Broader SCID genetic-etiology studies place CD3E among the causal genes identified via next-generation sequencing in national SCID cohorts, e.g., a Turkish series characterizing the "mutational landscape of severe combined immunodeficiency patients."

## 3. Biology Highlights
- CD3-chain deficiencies were characterized through "the molecular characterization of inherited T-cell immunodeficiencies," which found that "homozygous mutations in CD3D and CD3E genes lead to a complete block in" T-cell receptor complex assembly and T-cell development.
- CD3-epsilon is an obligate structural component of the TCR/CD3 complex required for surface TCR expression and T-cell receptor signal transduction — the core receptor-proximal signaling machinery that a CD4+ T-cell perturb-seq platform is positioned to probe directly, since loss of CD3E abolishes TCR-dependent T-cell activation and development.
- CD3-delta deficiency, a related CD3-subunit disorder, was noted to produce "profound T-cell deple[tion]," reinforcing that CD3-subunit loss broadly causes severe T-lymphopenia regardless of which chain is affected.

## 4. Current Treatment
No FDA-approved disease-specific pharmacologic therapy for CD3E deficiency was identified in this pass; the standard-of-care approach for CD3-subunit SCID-type immunodeficiencies described in the literature is definitive treatment via hematopoietic stem cell transplantation (HSCT), consistent with general SCID management, though no dedicated FDA drug application specific to CD3E deficiency was found via the Drugs@FDA search performed for this dossier.

## 5. Regulatory & Trials Snapshot
A ClinicalTrials.gov search on the specific condition "CD3 deficiency severe combined immunodeficiency" returned 1 total trial; the broader condition "severe combined immunodeficiency" returned 115 total trials, indicating most interventional activity is registered under the general SCID umbrella rather than the CD3E-specific subtype.
