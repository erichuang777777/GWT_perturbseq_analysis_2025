Avoid High-Risk Target List (de-risk tool)

Complementary to the discovery tool above: this lists genes that should be used with caution or avoided as knockdown/inhibition targets.

⚠ High-risk targets (avoid list)
Purpose: these targets have high pleiotropy (pan-effect breadth) and essentiality (genetic constraint, dosage sensitivity), so knockdown or inhibition carries high off-target toxicity risk; in clinical translation they should be carefully evaluated or avoided as direct therapeutic targets. This panel is for de-risking and complements the "Risk × Evidence" discovery tool above — one finds candidates, the other excludes high-risk candidates. Example: MED12 — LOEUF 0.10 (very high genetic constraint) + pLI 1.00 (dosage-sensitive) + pan-effect breadth top 10%; all three risk flags hit.
GeneTierRisk signals (hits) LOEUFpLIBreadth (max DE)
Tier: avoid = all three risk flags hit (pan-effect + high genetic constraint + dosage-sensitive); high_risk = ≥2 risk flags hit. Risk-flag definitions: pan-effect = breadth in the top 10%; high genetic constraint = gnomAD LOEUF < 0.35; dosage-sensitive = pLI ≥ 0.9. This list is a population-level target de-risking guide, not a patient-level prediction, and does not constitute medical advice for any specific patient. The Context-specific tag indicates that the gene shows a strong effect only in a specific population context.
